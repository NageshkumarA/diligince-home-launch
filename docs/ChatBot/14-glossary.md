# Glossary

A comprehensive list of terms and definitions used in the Diligince.ai platform.

---

## A

### Approval Matrix
A configuration that defines the hierarchy of approvers who must review and approve a requirement before it can be published. Consists of multiple levels with assigned approvers.

### Approval Level
A tier in the approval matrix. Requirements move through levels sequentially (Level 1 → Level 2 → Level 3, etc.).

### Approver
A person designated to review and approve/reject requirements at a specific level in the approval matrix.

### Active PO
A purchase order that has been accepted by the vendor and is currently being fulfilled.

### Active Requirement
A requirement that is published and open for vendor quotations.

### Archived
A status indicating a requirement, quotation, or PO has been closed and moved to historical records.

---

## B

### Budget
The estimated or allocated amount for a procurement activity.

### Budget Utilization
The percentage of allocated budget that has been spent or committed.

### Bulk Operations
Actions performed on multiple items at once (e.g., bulk approve, bulk reject).

---

## C

### Category
Classification of requirement type:
- **Professional Services**: Consulting, legal, accounting, etc.
- **Expert Services**: Specialized technical expertise
- **Product Requirements**: Physical goods and supplies

### Clarification
A request from buyer to vendor for additional information about a quotation.

### Completed
Status indicating all work, deliverables, and payments are finished.

### Cost Center
An organizational unit or code for tracking expenses.

### Cycle Time
The duration from requirement creation to completion (e.g., time to PO issuance).

---

## D

### Dashboard
The main overview page showing key metrics, pending actions, and quick access to features.

### Deliverable
A specific item, service, or output that a vendor must provide as part of a purchase order.

### Department
An organizational division or team within a company.

### Draft
An incomplete requirement saved for later editing. Not yet submitted for approval.

### Due Diligence
The process of evaluating vendors for capability, reliability, and risk.

---

## E

### Export
The ability to download data in formats like PDF, Excel, or CSV.

---

## F

### Filter
Options to narrow down list views based on criteria like status, date, vendor, etc.

---

## G

### GST/VAT
Goods and Services Tax / Value Added Tax - tax identifiers required for business transactions.

---

## H

### HUB (Diligince HUB)
The marketplace section where buyers discover and evaluate vendors and professionals.

---

## I

### Industry Admin
The highest-level role with full access to all features and settings.

### Industry User
A buyer on the platform representing a company that procures products/services.

### Invoice
A document submitted by vendor requesting payment for goods/services delivered.

### In Progress
Status indicating work is actively being performed on a PO.

---

## K

### KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
Metrics used to measure and track performance (e.g., cycle time, budget variance).

---

## L

### Line Item
Individual products or services listed in a quotation or purchase order.

---

## M

### Milestone
A checkpoint in a purchase order, often tied to a payment trigger.

### Milestone Payment
A payment made upon completion of a specific milestone.

### Module
A functional section of the platform (e.g., Requirements, Quotations, Settings).

---

## N

### Notification
Alert sent to users about important events or required actions.

---

## O

### On-time Delivery
A metric measuring the percentage of deliveries completed by the agreed date.

---

## P

### Pending
Status indicating an item is awaiting action (approval, review, acceptance).

### Pending Approval
A requirement or other item submitted and waiting for approver review.

### Permission
A specific access right (Read, Write, Edit, Delete, Download) for a module.

### PO (Purchase Order)
A formal document issued to a vendor confirming the purchase of products/services.

### Portfolio
A collection of past projects or work samples shown on a vendor's profile.

### Priority
The urgency level of a requirement:
- **Critical**: Immediate attention required
- **High**: Urgent
- **Medium**: Normal priority
- **Low**: Can wait

### Procurement
The process of finding, evaluating, and acquiring products or services.

### Professional
An individual expert or consultant offering services on the platform.

### Profile
The public or private page containing information about a user, company, or vendor.

### Published
A requirement status indicating it is live and visible to vendors.

---

## Q

### Quote / Quotation
A vendor's response to a requirement, including proposed pricing and terms.

### Quote Validity
The period during which a quotation's terms and pricing are guaranteed.

---

## R

### RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)
A security model where access rights are defined by roles, not individual users.

### Requirement
A formal document specifying what products or services are needed, used to solicit vendor quotations.

### Requirement ID
A unique identifier for each requirement (e.g., REQ-2024-001).

### Role
A defined set of permissions assigned to users (e.g., Industry Admin, Procurement Manager).

---

## S

### Scope of Work (SOW)
A document describing the work to be performed under a contract.

### Session
An active login instance. Multiple sessions can exist across different devices.

### Settings
Configuration area for company and user preferences.

### Status
The current state of an item (Draft, Pending, Active, Completed, etc.).

### Submodule
A section within a main module (e.g., "Create New" under "Requirements").

---

## T

### Team Member
An employee or user who is part of a company's account on the platform.

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
An extra security layer requiring a verification code in addition to password.

---

## U

### User
Any person with an account on the platform.

### User Type
Classification of users:
- **Industry**: Buyers
- **Vendor**: Sellers
- **Professional**: Individual experts

---

## V

### Vendor
A company or organization that provides products or services.

### Vendor Performance
Metrics measuring a vendor's reliability, quality, and delivery record.

### Verification
The process of confirming identity, documents, or account details.

### Verified
Status indicating a user, vendor, or piece of information has been confirmed.

---

## W

### Workflow
A sequence of steps or processes (e.g., approval workflow).

---

## X

### XLSX
Microsoft Excel file format used for data export.
